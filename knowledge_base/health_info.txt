# Comprehensive Health Information for Seniors

## Common Health Conditions

### Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
- **Symptoms**: Often no symptoms, but may include headaches, dizziness, blurred vision, chest pain, shortness of breath.
- **Causes**: Age, family history, obesity, lack of exercise, high salt intake, stress, smoking, alcohol.
- **Treatment**: Lifestyle changes (diet, exercise), medications like ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, diuretics.
- **Prevention**: Maintain healthy weight, reduce salt, exercise regularly, limit alcohol, manage stress.
- **When to see a doctor**: If BP > 140/90 mmHg consistently.

### Diabetes
- **Types**: Type 1 (autoimmune), Type 2 (lifestyle-related), Gestational.
- **Symptoms**: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, hunger, fatigue, slow-healing sores, blurred vision, tingling in hands/feet.
- **Causes**: Insulin resistance, genetics, obesity, sedentary lifestyle.
- **Treatment**: Diet, exercise, oral medications, insulin injections.
- **Prevention**: Healthy diet, regular exercise, weight management.
- **Complications**: Heart disease, kidney damage, nerve damage, eye problems.

### Arthritis
- **Types**: Osteoarthritis (wear and tear), Rheumatoid (autoimmune).
- **Symptoms**: Joint pain, stiffness, swelling, reduced range of motion.
- **Causes**: Age, injury, genetics, autoimmune disorders.
- **Treatment**: Pain relievers, anti-inflammatory drugs, physical therapy, joint replacement.
- **Prevention**: Maintain healthy weight, exercise, avoid joint injury.

### Heart Disease
- **Types**: Coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias.
- **Symptoms**: Chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, dizziness, palpitations.
- **Causes**: High cholesterol, hypertension, smoking, diabetes, family history.
- **Treatment**: Lifestyle changes, medications, surgery (bypass, stents).
- **Prevention**: Healthy diet, exercise, no smoking, manage stress.

## Medications and Side Effects

### Common Medications
- **Aspirin**: Prevents blood clots, side effects: stomach upset, bleeding risk.
- **Statins**: Lower cholesterol, side effects: muscle pain, liver damage.
- **Beta-blockers**: Lower BP and heart rate, side effects: fatigue, cold hands/feet.
- **ACE inhibitors**: Lower BP, side effects: cough, dizziness.
- **Metformin**: Diabetes control, side effects: nausea, diarrhea.

### Drug Interactions
- Avoid mixing blood thinners with NSAIDs.
- Consult doctor before taking supplements with prescriptions.
- Alcohol can interact with many medications.

## Nutrition and Diet

### Balanced Diet for Seniors
- **Fruits and Vegetables**: 5+ servings daily for vitamins and fiber.
- **Whole Grains**: For energy and digestion.
- **Lean Proteins**: Fish, poultry, beans for muscle maintenance.
- **Dairy**: Low-fat for calcium and vitamin D.
- **Hydration**: 8+ glasses of water daily.

### Special Diets
- **Low-Sodium**: For hypertension.
- **Low-Carb**: For diabetes management.
- **Heart-Healthy**: Mediterranean diet with olive oil, nuts, fish.

## Exercise and Physical Activity

### Benefits
- Improves cardiovascular health, strengthens bones, boosts mood, maintains weight.

### Recommended Activities
- Walking, swimming, yoga, light strength training.
- Aim for 150 minutes moderate exercise per week.

### Precautions
- Consult doctor before starting new routine.
- Start slow, warm up, cool down.
- Stop if experiencing pain or dizziness.

## Mental Health

### Common Issues
- Depression, anxiety, dementia, Alzheimer's.

### Signs
- Persistent sadness, loss of interest, memory loss, confusion.

### Support
- Therapy, medication, support groups, lifestyle changes.
- Stay socially active, learn new things.

## Preventive Care

### Screenings
- Annual physical exams.
- Blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes tests.
- Cancer screenings (mammogram, colonoscopy).

### Vaccinations
- Flu shot annually.
- Pneumonia vaccine.
- Shingles vaccine.

### Healthy Habits
- Don't smoke, limit alcohol, get enough sleep (7-9 hours).
- Manage stress through relaxation techniques.

## Emergency Situations

### When to Call Emergency
- Chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe headache, sudden weakness, confusion.
- Falls with injury, uncontrolled bleeding.

### First Aid Basics
- CPR if trained, stop bleeding with pressure, treat shock by keeping warm.

## General Health Tips
- Listen to your body, report changes to doctor.
- Keep medical records organized.
- Take medications as prescribed.
- Stay informed about health conditions.
- Build a support network of family and friends.

This knowledge base covers a wide range of health topics relevant to seniors. For personalized advice, always consult a healthcare professional.
